A real client, a real deadline, and a real brief — build an online presence for an African food business that makes customers feel the warmth of the food before they even order. This was my first project where someone else's livelihood was attached to what I shipped. I built the full stack with auth, so  the owner(client) could manage orders. It changed how seriously I take production-ready code.

What made it hard?

Translating a client's vision into a working product without a design handoff. I had to ask the right questions, make judgment calls, and deliver something they hadn't fully articulated. That gap between "what they said" and "what they meant" is where most of the real work happened.
